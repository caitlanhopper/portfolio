CAIT HOPPER

UXR & FASHION FUTURES

My perfect work day would be using the design thinking process as part of a team to ideate and conduct user experience research. I’m currently a second year Bachelor of Design student at QUT. I'm double majoring in Interaction Design and Fashion Communication to work towards a job where I can have my dream work day everyday.

I’m currently looking for a UX internship part time during semester or full time over the summer, either in Brisbane or fully remote.

Throughout my degree I’ve researched and created projects aimed at improving Brisbane CBD, helping young adults reach Kids Helpline and encouraging high school girls to consider careers in STEM. I also often explore my personal interest, the intersection of technology and fashion. I’ve prototyped apps to help people curate a slow fashion wardrobe, and prototyped a tool to help surface designers create visually interesting digital motifs through noise distortion using JavaScript.

I love being part of QUT’s fast paced start-up scene at The Foundry, having participated in both SPARK and FUEL entrepreneurship programs. I also pride myself on my academic achievements having recently made it onto the Creative Industries Dean’s List.

When I’m not working with tech I’m usually nerding out over something textiles related: I enjoy weaving, crochet and sewing my own clothes but am just as happy researching the latest textile advancements. I’m fascinated by the fashion industry and would love to be part of helping it transition to a sustainable and ethical model.
