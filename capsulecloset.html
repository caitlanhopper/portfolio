THE PROBLEM

Post consumer fashion waste is a huge problem in Australia with Australians throwing out approximately 6000 kgs of clothing every ten minutes.

Part of this waste comes from people making poor buying choices and not being aware of what already exists in their overfilled wardrobes.

THE OUTCOME

The final solution was a closet curation app to help women choose seasonal capsule wardrobes around a limited colour palette. They are able to use the app to create numerous outfit combinations. The app aims to reduce waste by showing users that there are many outfit combination opportunities and encourages them to keep their clothing in circulation for longer.

I chose the colour palette for the app by researching aesthetically pleasing wardrobe collections on Instagram. I looked at what sort of colours were being consistently presented by lifestyle influencers in the 30 to 40 age bracket

I felt that a very simple, paired back colour palette would be calming and create a feel of high end comfort

Buttons were styled with square photos that had clearly been carefully framed. This was meant to increase the feel that the app would be something used by influencers and to accentuate the feeling that all content had been curated

REFLECTION

This project was completed in a very limited time frame (a single weekend) and so the design process was greatly condensed. In an ideal scenario my process would include:

  • More User Research: In person or Zoom observations so that I could ask users clarifying questions around their storage and purchasing decisions in the moment.
  • More Iterations / Ideation: I would normally spend more time ideating using processes such as Crazy Eights, storyboarding and more lo-fi prototypes that I would ask for user feedback on.
  • User Testing and Stakeholder Feedback: I’d like to surface my assumptions by conducting more user testing and consulting with stakeholders, such as devs, throughout the design process to test for feasibility.
  • Collaboration: UX Design and Research are both collaborative processes. Normally I’d like to bounce ideas off a diverse team of users, stakeholders and designers to ensure we are creating a product that really solves a problem for users, is human centred and that is fully functional as well as aesthetically pleasing.
